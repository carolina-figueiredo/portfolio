Descarrilados.

Video Mapping Project.

For this project, my team and I created a Video Mapping with the purpose of celebrating the 50th anniversary of the 1974 Portugal Revolution. With this revolution the Salazars regime ended and if we look back in time we realize that nowadays we take things for granted, such as the simple act of kissing in public or playing cards on a train. Those two themes were the ones we chose to make this video mapping about.


The animation features six characters, each carefully designed. These characters form two contrasting groups: the King, Queen, and two men on the train represent the Portuguese people's yearning for freedom, while the Jack and the PIDE officer represent the oppressive regime.


The short story unfolds across three distinct settings, each carefully crafted to deepen the narrative. The first setting is a detailed recreation of Lisbon's Santa Apolónia station in the 1950s, emphasizing the iconic “Foguete” train that traveled between Lisbon and Porto in 1953. The second environment is a lively, cartoonish bar with a vibrant, vintage aesthetic, capturing the playful spirit of the card world. Finally, a maze connects the real and card worlds, designed with specialized shaders to keep a cohesive cartoonish style. Here, the central characters—the King, Queen, and Jack—come to life, adding a whimsical touch to the visual narrative.
